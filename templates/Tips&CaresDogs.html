Dog care tips

Things you might know if you want to adopt a dog

1. Evaluate your lifestyle

Evaluate your daily routine, living space, and financial resources to determine the type of dog that best fits your lifestyle. Different breeds and ages have varying energy levels and care requirements, and in order to do this we recommend you to do our socioeconomic study to find your perfect pawl that fits in your lifestyle.

2. Prepare your home

Remember that your pet is getting used to his new human family as much as you are getting used to your new pet. Create a safe and comfortable environment by removing hazards and setting up designated areas for feeding, sleeping, and play.

3. Veterinary check-Up

Arrange an initial health examination to assess the dog's health, update vaccinations, and discuss a preventive care plan.

4. Feeding

Every different type of dog needs a different alimentation, feed your dog a balanced diet appropriate for its age, size, and activity level. Consult your veterinarian for specific dietary recommendations.

5. Excercise

Dogs need exercise to burn calories, stimulate their minds, and stay healthy. Individual exercise needs vary based on breed or breed mix, sex, age and level of health. Supervised fun and games will satisfy many of your pet's instinctual urges to dig, herd, chew, retrieve and chase.

6. Get indentification

Sadly but true, all too often dogs go missing, so be prepared and get a collar with personalized tags with your dogs name, your address, and your phone number.

7. Maintain Grooming and Hygiene

Regularly groom your dog, including brushing, bathing, and dental care, to maintain overall health and cleanliness, note that every dog have differents needs, so consult your veterinary for specific recommendations.

8. Understand the commitment

Recognize that adopting a dog is a long-term commitment, often spanning 10 to 15 years or more. So be prepared for the responsibilities and changes this entails.
