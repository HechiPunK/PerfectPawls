Cat care tips

Things you might know if you want to adopt a cat.

1. Consider Your Lifestyle

Cats are generally more independent than dogs but still require daily interaction, playtime, and routine. If you travel a lot or work long hours, consider whether you will be able to provide the daily companionship and care they need.

2. Prepare your home

Cats love to explore, climb, hide and scratch, so creating a safe space with cozy beds, scratching posts, and perches can help them feel secure. Ensure breakables are out of reach, cover electrical cords, and secure any small items that could be swallowed.

3. Veterinary check-Up

Arrange an initial health examination to assess the cat's health, update vaccinations, and discuss a preventive care plan.

4. Quality Food and Fresh Water

Cats have unique dietary needs and are obligate carnivores, meaning they require a diet high in protein. Consider consulting a vet to find food that fits your cats age, health, and any special needs they may have.

5. Keep Their Litter Box Clean

Cats are particular about cleanliness, especially when it comes to their litter box. Clean it daily, and completely replace the litter weekly. Ideally, provide one litter box per cat, plus an extra one, and place them in private, easily accessible locations.

6. Get indentification

Sadly but true, all too often cats go missing, so be prepared and get a collar with personalized tags with your cats name, your address, and your phone number.

7. Brushing

brushing keeps your cat's coat healthy, reduces shedding, and prevents hairballs. Brush short-haired cats weekly, and long-haired cats daily to avoid painful mats. Use a slicker brush or grooming glove, and turn it into a bonding activity by going slow and rewarding them with treats.

8. Understand the commitment

Cats can be with you for two decades or more, so make sure you are ready for the long haul. Consider how changes in your life, like moves, relationships, or job changes, might affect your ability to care for them.
